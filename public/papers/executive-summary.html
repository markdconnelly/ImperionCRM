The thesis: an OS for agents

An operating system schedules processes over shared resources under governed access. Imperion OS does exactly this — but the processes are AI agents, and the shared resource is everything the business knows and can do. The metaphor is not decoration; it is the design constraint that produced the architecture.

Two claims, woven together

Data-as-kernel. The data platform is the kernel. It owns provenance, meaning, retrieval, and permissions — the things an agent must be able to trust before it reasons or acts. Agents never touch raw resources directly; they go through the kernel's governed interfaces. A fact carries its source and capture time, so "says who, and as of when?" — the first question any agent should be able to answer — is answerable by construction.

Second-brain-as-OS. The knowledge tiers are the agent's memory hierarchy — a second brain whose recall is identity-scoped, citation-backed, and consolidated by a background process, the way a real OS pages memory and a real brain consolidates during sleep. A personal brain cannot leak into the company tier — the boundary is a database invariant, not a code convention.

One loop, three altitudes

It is one architecture, not three add-ons. The same refinement DNA — trust is earned in stages, never declared — appears at three altitudes:

Altitude 1 · data
Medallion

raw → trustworthy → AI-ready

Altitude 2 · meaning
OKF / IKF

observed → defined → authoritative

Altitude 3 · action
ICM / MWP

drafted → approved → auto

Each shares four traits: progressive refinement, plain-text and version-controlled, human-readable = machine-readable, and human-in-the-loop by default with autonomy as a dial. Writes flow back to source systems and re-enter as bronze — a closed, auditable loop.

Why the autonomy is safe

Trust to read is the data half; trust to act is the action half. Raising autonomy is only safe because four governance planes hold at once:

  • Trust to act — deny-by-default action / tool-grants with evaluated scopes; a prompt-injected agent cannot reach a tool it was never granted.
  • Metered action — a 1–5 autonomy dial; anything above an agent's ceiling routes to a native approval cockpit instead of executing.
  • Proof of what happened — an append-only agent_run / agent_message ledger; every grant decision is auditable.
  • Proof it was right — an eval / quality plane that gates regressions in CI against committed baselines.

Honest limits. This is not "one autonomous AI that just runs the business" — by design it is staged, contracted, and gated; reframe "fully autonomous" as "fully automated with supervision you can dial." It is near-real-time (webhooks + on-demand refresh), not streaming analytics. And the curated meaning layer is load-bearing: a stale semantic layer would lie with confidence, which is why freshness is enforced as a CI gate.
