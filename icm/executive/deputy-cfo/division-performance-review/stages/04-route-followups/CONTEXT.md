# Stage 04 — route-followups

**Job:** route each flag — a business gap is delegated to the owning report; a
governance concern is handed up to Jessica/Vera/Tess — then park. Sterling never
adjudicates a governance concern, never changes a dial, never actuates.

## Inputs

| Source | Location | Scope | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief | stage 03 `brief.md` | the two flag lists | what to route |

## Process

1. `[sonnet]` For each grounded **business gap**, draft a **proposed** `delegate()`
   to the **owning report** (Chase / Belle / Celeste / Vance / Audrey / Bridget): the
   report agent_key, the outcome that is lagging, and the corrective ask. The report
   acts under its own gauntlet — nothing is composed or actuated here.
2. `[sonnet]` For each **governance concern** (sliding eval, mis-set dial,
   conformance/quality drift), draft a `handoff()` to **Jessica** (CRO) — or Vera
   (conformance) / Tess (service-quality) as the lens fits — naming the report and
   the concern. Sterling does **not** adjudicate it and does **not** recommend a rung;
   the dial decision is the CRO's lane.
3. `[script]` Attach each delegate/handoff to the brief as a proposal and park. No
   send, no write, no dial change — the corrective happens inside the report, the
   governance call inside the CRO's lane.

## Outputs

`followups.md` — the proposed `delegate()` calls to the owning reports and the
`handoff()` calls to Jessica/Vera/Tess, each citing the report and the grounded flag.
The run ends here at the checkpoint; nothing is actuated.
