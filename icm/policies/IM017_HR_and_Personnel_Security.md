## 1. Purpose

To establish personnel-security controls across the employment lifecycle — screening, onboarding, ongoing responsibility, and disciplinary process — so that the people entrusted with Imperion and client information are suitable, informed, and accountable.

## 2. Definitions

- **Workforce Member** — Any employee, contractor, or consultant with access to Imperion or client systems or data.
- **Screening** — Pre-employment verification appropriate to the role and access level.
- **Sanctions** — Disciplinary measures for policy violations.

## 3. Statement of Policy

### 3.1 Pre-Employment Screening

- Background screening is performed before granting access, proportional to the sensitivity of the role and data access, and consistent with applicable law.
- Screening may include identity verification, employment/education verification, criminal background checks, and, for privileged or financially sensitive roles, additional checks.
- Contractors and consultants with access are screened to an equivalent standard, or covered by contractual assurances from their employer (IM013).

### 3.2 Onboarding (Joiner)

- New workforce members complete security and privacy awareness training before or upon receiving access (IM010, Enterprise Program §5).
- Each new member signs acknowledgment of the Acceptable Use Policy (IM010), confidentiality/non-disclosure obligations, and applicable policies.
- Access is provisioned under least privilege per role (IM003/IM008).

### 3.3 Confidentiality and Roles

- All workforce members are bound by confidentiality obligations covering Imperion and client information, persisting beyond the end of employment.
- Security responsibilities are defined in role descriptions; individuals in security-relevant roles understand their specific obligations (GV.RR).

### 3.4 Ongoing Obligations

- Annual security awareness training and attestation are required (Enterprise Program §5).
- Workforce members report suspected security incidents promptly (Technical Incident Response Program).

### 3.5 Role Change (Mover)

- When a workforce member changes role, access is re-evaluated and adjusted to the new role under least privilege; access no longer required is removed promptly (IM008).

### 3.6 Separation (Leaver)

- Offboarding follows IM007, including timely access revocation, token/session invalidation, device and credential recovery, and 1Password de-provisioning.

### 3.7 Sanctions and Disciplinary Process

- Violations of security policy are subject to a documented disciplinary process up to and including termination, applied consistently and in coordination with HR and Legal.
- Where violations involve client data or legal obligations, reporting to clients or authorities occurs as required.
