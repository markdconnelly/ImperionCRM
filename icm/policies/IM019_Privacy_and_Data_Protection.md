## 1. Purpose

To govern the responsible collection, use, disclosure, retention, and protection of personal data, and to define how Imperion meets its privacy obligations as both a controller of its own data and a processor acting on behalf of clients.

## 2. Definitions

- **Personal Data / PII** — Information relating to an identified or identifiable individual.
- **Sensitive Personal Data** — Categories warranting heightened protection (e.g., health, financial, biometric, government identifiers).
- **Controller** — The party determining the purposes and means of processing personal data.
- **Processor** — A party processing personal data on behalf of a controller. Imperion typically acts as a processor for client data.
- **Data Subject** — The individual to whom personal data relates.

## 3. Statement of Policy

### 3.1 Privacy Principles

Imperion processes personal data in accordance with recognized privacy principles: lawfulness and transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality, and accountability.

### 3.2 Roles — Controller vs. Processor

- For Imperion's own data (employees, prospects, vendors), Imperion acts as controller and processes data for defined business purposes.
- For client data, Imperion acts as a processor and processes personal data only on documented client instruction and within the scope of the service agreement and any Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

### 3.3 Lawful and Limited Processing

- Personal data is collected and used only for legitimate, specified purposes and is not repurposed without basis.
- Data minimization applies — only the personal data necessary for the purpose is processed.
- Personal data is not entered into AI tools except as permitted by IM010/IM011 and the applicable classification (IM012).

### 3.4 Data Subject Rights

- Where applicable law or client agreement grants data subject rights (access, correction, deletion, etc.), Imperion supports the controller in responding within required timeframes.
- For client data, requests received by Imperion are referred to the client controller unless the agreement directs otherwise.

### 3.5 Cross-Border and Residency

- Personal data is processed and stored within the United States unless otherwise contractually approved (IM009), with appropriate safeguards for any cross-border transfer.

### 3.6 Retention and Disposal

- Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for its purpose or as required by law/contract, then securely disposed (IM012, IM024).

### 3.7 Breach Notification

- Privacy incidents involving personal data are handled under the Technical Incident Response Program.
- Affected controllers (clients), data subjects, and regulators are notified as required by applicable law and contract, within mandated timeframes (vendor/processor notification no later than 30 days where applicable — IM009/IM013).

### 3.8 Vendor and Sub-Processor Privacy

- Vendors and sub-processors handling personal data are assessed and contractually bound to equivalent privacy and security obligations (IM013), including DPAs where required.
