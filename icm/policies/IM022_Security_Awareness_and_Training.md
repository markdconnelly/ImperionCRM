## 1. Purpose

To ensure all workforce members understand their security responsibilities and have the knowledge to protect Imperion and client information, building a security-aware culture consistent with "security is our reputation."

## 2. Statement of Policy

### 2.1 New-Hire Training

- All new workforce members complete security and privacy awareness training before or upon receiving access, covering acceptable use (IM010), data handling (IM012), phishing recognition, incident reporting, and AI use.

### 2.2 Annual Training and Attestation

- All workforce members complete annual security awareness training and pass an assessment attesting to their understanding of policy.
- Completion is tracked and recorded as audit evidence.

### 2.3 Quarterly Reminders

- The CISO issues quarterly security reminders themed to current threats and trends drawn from incident and phishing-simulation data.

### 2.4 Role-Based Training

- Workforce members in privileged, technical, or developer roles receive additional role-specific training (e.g., secure configuration, secure use of AI tools, privileged-access handling).

### 2.5 Phishing Simulation

- Recurring phishing-simulation testing (Kaseya) measures susceptibility and reinforces training; results inform targeted follow-up (IM021).

### 2.6 AI Awareness

- Training includes responsible and secure use of AI tools, including what data may never be entered into AI services (IM010/IM011).

### 2.7 Effectiveness and Improvement

- Training content and frequency are reviewed at least annually and adjusted based on the threat landscape, incident trends, and regulatory change.
- Awareness metrics (completion rates, phishing-simulation results) are reported to the CISO.
