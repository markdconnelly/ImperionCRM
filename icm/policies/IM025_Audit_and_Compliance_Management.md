## 1. Purpose

To define how Imperion maintains, evidences, and demonstrates compliance with its security program, applicable frameworks (NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2), and contractual/regulatory obligations through structured audit and compliance management.

## 2. Definitions

- **Control Owner** — The individual accountable for the operation and evidence of a specific control.
- **Evidence** — Records demonstrating that a control operated as intended.
- **Audit** — An independent assessment of control design and operating effectiveness.
- **Finding** — A deficiency or gap identified through audit or monitoring.

## 3. Statement of Policy

### 3.1 Framework Alignment

- The security program is mapped to **NIST CSF 2.0** and the **SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria**; each control objective is owned and evidenced.
- A control matrix maps policies (IM-series) to framework requirements and control owners, maintained by Information Security.

### 3.2 Control Ownership and Evidence

- Each control has an assigned owner responsible for its operation and for retaining evidence.
- Evidence is collected on a defined cadence and stored in a manner suitable for audit (tamper-resistant, access-controlled).

### 3.3 Internal Audit and Self-Assessment

- Internal control self-assessments are performed on a defined schedule to verify controls are operating effectively.
- Results are documented; deficiencies become findings tracked to remediation.

### 3.4 External Audit (SOC 2)

- Imperion supports independent SOC 2 examination, coordinating evidence collection, auditor access, and remediation.
- The scope, period, and Trust Services Criteria for each examination are defined with the auditor.

### 3.5 Findings and Corrective Action

- Findings are risk-rated (IM006), assigned an owner, and tracked to closure with target dates.
- Material findings are reported to executive leadership.

### 3.6 Continuous Monitoring Linkage

- Compliance status draws on continuous monitoring (IM014); exceptions detected through monitoring are recorded as incidents with documented risk assessment.

### 3.7 Regulatory and Contractual Compliance

- Applicable laws, regulations, and client contractual obligations are tracked; changes are assessed for impact on the program (Legal coordination).

### 3.8 Reporting and Oversight

- Compliance posture, audit results, and remediation status are reported to executive leadership at least annually, and more frequently for material matters (GV.OV).
