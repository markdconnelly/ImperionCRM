## 1. Purpose

This policy documents the overarching strategy for information security at Imperion. As a managed service provider (MSP) entrusted with the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of our own and our clients' information, security is the foundation of our reputation and our business. This strategy establishes the governance model under which all subordinate policies, standards, and procedures operate, and commits Imperion to compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations across people, process, and technology.

## 2. Strategic Principles

Imperion's security program is built on the following principles:

- **Security is non-negotiable.** Security is our reputation. No business objective, delivery deadline, or cost consideration overrides our commitment to protecting information assets — our own or our clients'.
- **Secure by design and by default.** Security controls are built into systems, services, and client engagements from inception rather than added retroactively.
- **Zero Trust as the operating model.** No user, device, or network is trusted by default. Every access request is explicitly verified, granted with least privilege, and assumes breach.
- **Defense in depth.** Layered administrative, technical, and physical controls ensure that the failure of any single control does not result in compromise.
- **Continuous improvement.** The program is measured, audited, and adapted to changes in the threat landscape, technology, regulation, and business objectives.
- **Responsible innovation.** Imperion is a premier vendor for incorporating emerging technology, including artificial intelligence, into client environments. We adopt innovation deliberately, with security and governance controls applied before deployment, never after.

## 3. Definitions

- **Information Security** — The governance discipline of securing sensitive information in all forms (electronic, print, and spoken) through physical, technical, and administrative controls. The function includes auditing and monitoring activities that ensure ongoing compliance with regulation, corporate policy, and industry best practice. At Imperion, Information Security is a function of the Security Operations group and reports to the CISO.
- **Cybersecurity** — The technical discipline of securing electronic information and information assets (servers, workstations, mobile devices, cloud workloads, and network infrastructure). Cybersecurity owns the architecture, deployment, and maintenance of the technical security stack and acts as first responder during security incidents. At Imperion, Cybersecurity is a function of Security Operations and reports to the CISO.
- **Security Operations (SecOps)** — The combined Information Security and Cybersecurity function responsible for day-to-day monitoring, detection, response, and control operation across Imperion and client environments.
- **Enterprise Information Security Program** — The documented program (see Enterprise Information Security Program) that defines the control objectives, policies, and processes implementing this strategy.

## 4. Statement of Policy

### 4.1 The Enterprise Information Security Program

As part of this strategy, Imperion maintains an Enterprise Information Security Program. The program consists of, but is not limited to:

- Policies, standards, and procedures
- Personnel and physical security
- Information systems management and access control
- Asset classification and data handling
- Secure system development, deployment, and maintenance
- Risk analysis and risk management
- Third-party and supply-chain risk management
- Artificial intelligence governance and secure AI deployment
- Administrative, technical, and physical safeguards
- Vulnerability assessment and remediation
- Logging, monitoring, detection, and response
- Backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity
- Security awareness, training, and phishing simulation

### 4.2 Governance and Decision Authority

The security program is designed to provide reasonable and appropriate safeguards for the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information and information assets. Information Security and Cybersecurity work in cooperation to ensure the program is applied consistently across Imperion's internal operations and all client engagements.

Security decisions follow a defined chain:

1. **Technical feasibility** — Cybersecurity assesses whether a control or change is technically achievable.
2. **Policy alignment** — Information Security assesses policy and compliance implications.
3. **Legal review** — Legal assesses contractual, regulatory, and liability considerations.
4. **Management approval** — Executive leadership provides sign-off proportional to the risk.

### 4.3 Purpose of the Program

The Enterprise Information Security Program exists to:

- Establish policies, procedures, plans, and tooling that secure information in compliance with applicable state, federal, and international requirements and the SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria.
- Support Imperion's mission to deliver continuous, secure managed services to its clients.
- Maintain unbroken trust with employees, clients, and stakeholders through demonstrable stewardship of information assets.

### 4.4 Ownership and Maintenance

The Enterprise Information Security Program is owned by the CISO and is reviewed and revised at least annually, and as needed, to remain compliant with all applicable laws and regulations and aligned with business goals. Each department is responsible for compliance with this strategy and its subordinate policies.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Executive Leadership | Owns business initiatives requiring security controls; approves strategy; provides risk-treatment decisions above defined thresholds |
| CISO | Owns the security program and this strategy; reports risk to leadership; final authority on security policy |
| Cybersecurity | Architects and operates technical security solutions; first responder for incidents |
| Information Security | Defines security strategy and policy; maintains the documented program; performs audit and monitoring |
| Legal | Enforces applicable law; reviews contracts and regulatory obligations |
| All Workforce Members | Comply with all security policies; complete required training; report suspected incidents |

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance with this policy and all subordinate policies is mandatory. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract, and where applicable, civil or criminal referral. All Imperion and client network activity is subject to audit to verify compliance.
