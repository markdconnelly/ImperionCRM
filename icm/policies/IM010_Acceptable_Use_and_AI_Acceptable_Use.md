## 1. Purpose

To define acceptable and prohibited use of Imperion's information systems, networks, data, and — critically — artificial intelligence (AI) tools, ensuring that productivity-enhancing technology is used securely, ethically, and in compliance with client obligations.

## 2. Scope of "AI Tools"

This policy covers all forms of AI and machine-learning tools, including but not limited to: generative AI assistants (chat, code, image), AI features embedded in productivity suites (e.g., Microsoft 365 Copilot), AI coding assistants, AI features in the Kaseya platform, and any third-party or browser-based AI service.

## 3. General Acceptable Use

### 3.1 Permitted Use

- Imperion systems and accounts are provided for legitimate business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted only where it does not interfere with duties, consume material resources, or introduce risk.
- Users access only the data and systems for which they are authorized, under least privilege.

### 3.2 Prohibited Use

Users may **never**:

- Share, weaken, or circumvent authentication or security controls.
- Upload sensitive information to unapproved services or send it to personal accounts (see IM009).
- Install unauthorized software or connect unauthorized devices to managed networks.
- Use Imperion or client systems for unlawful, harassing, discriminatory, or fraudulent activity.
- Attempt to access, copy, or exfiltrate client data beyond the scope of an authorized engagement.

### 3.3 Monitoring

All activity on Imperion and managed networks is subject to logging, monitoring, and audit (IM014). Users should have no expectation of privacy in their use of company systems.

## 4. AI Acceptable Use

Imperion embraces AI as a productivity and service-delivery advantage, but only under disciplined governance. The following rules apply to all workforce use of AI tools.

### 4.1 Approved Tools Only

- Only AI tools that have been reviewed and approved by Information Security (and, where they process client data, accepted under IM011 and IM013) may be used for business work.
- "Shadow AI" — use of unapproved AI services for company or client work — is prohibited. A current list of approved AI tools is maintained by Information Security.

### 4.2 Data Protection — What May Never Be Entered

Workforce members must **never** input the following into any AI tool unless that specific tool is explicitly approved for that data class with appropriate contractual and technical protections (e.g., no-training guarantees, tenant isolation):

- Client sensitive information, credentials, secrets, or configuration that could enable compromise.
- Personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information, or financial data.
- Imperion trade secrets, security architecture details, incident details, or source code containing secrets.
- Any data whose classification (IM012) is Confidential or Restricted, into a consumer/public AI service.

When in doubt, do not enter it — ask Information Security.

### 4.3 Human Oversight and Accountability

- AI output is **assistive, not authoritative.** The workforce member remains fully accountable for any work product, decision, code, or client deliverable derived from AI.
- AI output must be reviewed for accuracy, security, licensing, and appropriateness before use. AI is known to produce confident but incorrect results ("hallucinations"); unverified output must not be delivered to clients or acted upon in production.
- AI must not be the sole basis for decisions that materially affect a person's rights, employment, security posture, or access.

### 4.4 Security and Code

- AI-generated code must be reviewed and tested before deployment and must not introduce secrets, vulnerable dependencies, or license violations.
- AI tools must not be used to generate or assist malicious code, to circumvent security controls, or to process another party's data without authorization.

### 4.5 Transparency and Ethics

- Where client contracts or law require disclosure of AI use, that disclosure is made.
- AI must be used in a manner consistent with fairness, non-discrimination, and Imperion's professional obligations.

### 4.6 Enterprise AI Governance

The deployment, configuration, and security of AI systems — including Microsoft 365 Copilot and any AI Imperion delivers to clients — is governed by **IM011 (AI Governance & Secure Deployment)**. This policy (IM010) governs individual workforce *use*; IM011 governs *systems and deployment*.

## 5. Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may be reported to clients or authorities where contractual or legal obligations require.
