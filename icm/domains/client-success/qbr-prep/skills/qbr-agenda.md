## The pack structure (the four sections, in order)

1. **Period recap** — what happened this quarter: service delivered (tickets resolved,
   SLAs met/missed), projects landed, value realized. Numbers with their source + as-of.
2. **Health & posture** — the current health read + the security posture summary (from
   Vera, via Celeste — advisory). Each verdict carries its measured signals.
3. **Forward agenda** — what the next quarter should prioritize: the initiatives, the
   refresh/roadmap items, the posture uplifts. Each framed as a *proposal* to discuss.
4. **Talking points & recommendations** — the discussion prompts + the parked
   recommendations the human facilitator will raise. Each tagged with its owner.

## Value narration (honest, not promotional)

- **Narrate value from signals, not adjectives.** "47 tickets resolved, 99.2% within SLA,
  the Exchange migration landed on time" — not "a great quarter." If the signals are
  mixed, say so; a QBR that hides a bad quarter is not advice.
- **A missed SLA or a major incident is on the pack.** Surfacing friction *is* the
  relationship work — never bury it to make the review comfortable.

## Discipline

- **Signal vs inference.** A health/value verdict carries the signals that produced it
  (celeste.md guardrail 3). Never invent value, sentiment, or momentum.
- **Propose, never commit.** Roadmap / SLA / pricing / spend / security-remediation in the
  pack are recommendations for the human facilitator, at every level (guardrail 1,
  dial-proof).
- **In the client's interest.** Flag a **non-interest upsell** explicitly (guardrail 4).
- **Expansion → Chase.** Real expansion value mints an opportunity for Chase, not a close
  in the review.
- **Security = advisory only.** Posture + recommendations; remediation is human / Datto
  (guardrail 2, MSSP boundary).
