# Stage 03 — draft-reply

**Job:** write one channel-correct, voice-compliant draft reply plus a short rationale.

## Inputs

| Source | Location | Scope | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dossier | `dossier.md` (stage 02 output) | full | the angle + what we know |
| Intent | `intent.md` (stage 01 output) | channel + public/DM flag | which channel rules apply |
| Voice | `../../skills/voice-and-tone.md` | all | every sales reply sounds the same |
| Social rules | `./skills/social-reply-rules.md` | the channel's section | hard channel constraints |

## Process

1. `[haiku]` Select the channel form per `social-reply-rules.md`: a **public comment** reply
   (concise, brand-safe, move detail to DM/email) vs a **private DM** (within the platform
   window; the page identity). Window expired on a DM → draft an email/booking-link fallback
   instead and note it.
2. `[sonnet]` Draft the reply on the chosen angle: answer what was asked, one CTA, ≤ the
   channel length cap. Never invent pricing, availability, or capability — pricing intent is
   a handling note, not numbers (Chase's guardrail). No fabricated urgency.
3. `[script]` Attach the rationale: angle, channel choice, and the consent/window basis.

## Outputs

`draft.md` — the draft reply, the channel form, and the rationale. Nothing is sent here.

## Audit

- [ ] One draft, one channel form, one CTA; within the channel length cap
- [ ] No invented pricing/availability/capability; no manufactured urgency
- [ ] Channel choice is legal per `social-reply-rules.md` (window, public-vs-DM, consent)
