# Skill (Tier 3, workflow-local): substantiation-rules

Mark-editable cite-or-cut rules for any claim in a Content Asset. Binds the persona's
"cite or don't claim" doctrine and CS-07 §5 into the compose and review stages. Stages
cite this; they never restate the policy. The principle: **a claim ships only with
substantiation on file; on empty evidence the claim is dropped or softened to honest
language — never invented.**

## What counts as a claim

Any assertion of fact about Imperion or its services in the asset: a capability, a result,
a metric, a certification, a customer outcome, a comparison, a scarcity/urgency statement.
In a **case_study** this includes every stated outcome; in a **battlecard** every
competitive claim; in a **press_release** every on-the-record fact.

## What counts as substantiation

A verifiable source on file: a measured metric (with its as-of), a held certification, a
**consented `reference`** (for any customer name, quote, testimonial, or outcome), a
documented result. **"We believe" / aspirational language is not substantiation** and may
not be phrased as fact.

## The rule, applied at compose time

1. For each claim, name its source (id / location) and as-of in the substantiation refs.
2. **No source → cut the claim or soften to honest language.** Do not invent a number, a
   testimonial, a quote, a logo, a customer name, or a result.
3. **A case-study claim must trace to a consented `reference`.** No customer name, quote,
   or outcome appears without one — an unconsented customer reference is a hard stop.
4. **No false urgency / scarcity** — "limited time", "only N left" only when literally true
   and substantiated.
5. **No impersonation, no fabricated quotes** — including in a press_release or announcement.
6. A claim with legal/compliance exposure (a competitive comparison, a certification, a
   guarantee) is escalated to the marketing lead, not self-approved.

A draft whose claims cannot all be sourced **parks** — that is the audit failure the
review-gate refuses (slop / an unsubstantiated published claim), and it is the failure mode
the brand refuses, not silence.
