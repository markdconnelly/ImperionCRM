# Skill (Tier 2, domain-shared): brand-voice

Mark-editable brand-voice reference for every marketing workflow. Promoted to the
domain tier because every public marketing word — a post, a reply, a campaign email —
must sound like one company. Workflows **cite** this; they never restate it. This is
business content (the *how we sound*), not a rule (rules live in policy §5 / the
persona). No claims, no client data here — examples only.

## The voice in one line

Polished, warm, and unmistakably human — a knowledgeable peer who respects the
reader's time, never a hype machine and never a press release.

## Always

- **On-brand and human over on-time and generic.** Ship less before shipping slop.
- **Plain, specific, confident.** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. Active voice.
- **Lead with the reader's problem,** not our product.
- **One idea per post,** one clear next step.

## Never

- **No fabricated stat, testimonial, quote, capability, or scarcity** — cite or cut
  (substantiation lives in `substantiation-rules.md`).
- **No hype, buzzword stacking, or AI-slop filler** ("leverage synergies",
  "in today's fast-paced world", "unlock", "supercharge").
- **No false urgency** — a clock only when the deadline is real.
- **No competitor disparagement, no client identities** in public copy.

## Per-channel register

The composition is authored once and adapted per network — a Threads reply does not
read like a LinkedIn post, and neither reads like a press release. The hard
per-channel constraints (length, format, hashtag/link norms) live in each workflow's
`channel-norms.md`; this file is the shared *tone*.
