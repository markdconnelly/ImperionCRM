# Skill (Tier 3, workflow-local): substantiation-rules

Mark-editable cite-or-cut rules for any claim in a Social Post. Binds the persona's
"cite or don't claim" doctrine and BO-01 §4.2 into the compose stage. Stages cite
this; they never restate the policy. The principle: **a claim ships only with
substantiation on file; on empty evidence the claim is dropped or softened to honest
language — never invented.**

## What counts as a claim

Any assertion of fact about Imperion or its services: a capability, a result, a
metric, a certification, a customer outcome, a comparison, a scarcity/urgency
statement.

## What counts as substantiation

A verifiable source on file: a measured metric (with its as-of), a held
certification, a signed reference/testimonial, a documented result. **"We believe" /
aspirational language is not substantiation** and may not be phrased as fact.

## The rule, applied at compose time

1. For each claim, name its source (id / location) and as-of in the rationale.
2. **No source → cut the claim or soften to honest language.** Do not invent a
   number, a testimonial, a quote, a logo, a customer name, or a result.
3. **No false urgency / scarcity** — "limited time", countdowns, "only N left" only
   when literally true and substantiated.
4. **No impersonation, no fabricated quotes.**
5. A claim with legal/compliance exposure (a comparison, a certification, a
   guarantee) is escalated to the marketing lead, not self-approved.

A draft whose claims cannot all be sourced **parks** — that is the audit failure, and
it is the failure mode the brand refuses (slop), not silence.
